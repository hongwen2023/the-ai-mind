The brief meets the Golden Chapter Standard. It is a Book-level Understanding
Audit rather than a summary, and tests whether the learner can reconstruct,
stress, repair, and transfer the relationship map from Chapters 1–8.

## Draft Guidance

1. Preserve Nodes, Edges, Direction, Boundary, Evidence, Failure Trace,
   Transfer, and Unknown as the Map Reconstruction Audit.
2. Introduce no new intelligence concept.
3. Require closed-book reconstruction, stress testing, and open-book repair.
4. Use $G=(V,E)$ only to express relationships; do not teach Graph Theory.
5. Keep the executable graph subordinate to relationship reasoning.
6. Transfer mechanisms, not vocabulary, in the Finance section.
7. State that a map is a task-relevant, testable relationship model rather

8. Include Wrong Edge failure and reverse Failure-to-Broken-Edge exercises.
